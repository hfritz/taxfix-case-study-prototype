01 · The segment

Not “self-employed.” This exact intersection.

Lena: a regelbesteuerte freelancer past the Kleinunternehmer line, with a client or income stream outside Germany. Either trait alone is already served today. Both at once is where Taxfix's product stops and a slow, expensive Steuerberater takes over.

Self-employed
Past the Kleinunternehmer line — regelbesteuert, not the small-business exemption.
+
Cross-border
A foreign client, foreign rental income, or a double-taxation-treaty case.

02 · The gap

Three weaknesses. Two are direct quotes, one is a judgment call.

Quote
“Gewerbetreibende mit Regelversteuerung” — named on Taxfix's own Experten-Service page as not supported.
Quote
“Einige internationale Steuerfälle” and Auslandsvermietung — also named exclusions, not an inference.
Inference
One annual filing only, not her real quarterly cadence. My own scoping read, not a documented gap — flagged, and kept out of v1 on purpose.

03 · The price

€449

Flat. 5 advisor hours included. A disclosed surcharge beyond that — never a surprise bill.

Doing it yourself
6–10 hours of your own time — worth €480–800 at €80/hr, plus the risk of a mistake.
A traditional Kanzlei
€520–1,500+ for a complex case, billed hourly. You don't know the total until it arrives.
This tier
€449, decided up front. Known before you start, not after.

04 · What's in, what's out

A complexity-based tier, not a “more of everything” one.

Included
  • A licensed Steuerberater with real self-employed + cross-border experience — not the generalist pool.
  • A structured intake that sizes complexity before any work starts.
  • One round of advisor follow-up and review before submission.
Deliberately excluded
  • Live or phone consultations — stays async to protect margin.
  • Quarterly VAT and bookkeeping — a different, bigger product. A roadmap note, not v1.
  • Multi-year retroactive filings.

The lever is complexity, priced honestly — not a bigger flat tier.

05 · The unit economics

gross margin at the base case — honest, and below the 40%+ bar this needs to reach once real hours-per-case data replaces the placeholder.

€349 only cleared 8.3% margin — too thin, so price moved to €449. The real risk isn't the price: it's hours-per-case, still a placeholder. Sensitivity analysis shows it swings margin more than advisor rate and opex combined (~€300, vs. ~€250 and ~€20).

07 · The AI workflow

Research, cross-checked. Not research, trusted.

Every model output in this deck was re-run somewhere else before being trusted.

NotebookLM ran a 6-phase interview-coach prompt against the real case brief before any solution was proposed.
The persona was defined once, then re-run independently in a second Claude session as a sanity check.
The price came from a Python sensitivity model, not a guess — and its first answer (€349) was corrected, not accepted.
Claude Code built all three pages end to end from the resulting specs.
This deck: built with a skill that samples Taxfix's own rebrand page directly for its palette, type, and layout rules — the same discipline applied everywhere else.
